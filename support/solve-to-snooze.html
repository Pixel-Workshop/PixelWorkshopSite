FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Make sure you have granted Solve To Snooze permission to send notifications. Go to iPhone Settings → Notifications → Solve To Snooze and ensure notifications are enabled and set to allow sounds and alerts. Also verify that your alarm is toggled on inside the app.
Check that your iPhone is not on silent mode (the physical mute switch on the side). Also verify that the volume is turned up. In the app, confirm that a sound is selected for the alarm rather than "None."
When creating or editing an alarm, you'll find a "Difficulty" option. Tap it to choose between Easy, Medium, and Hard. Easy uses simple addition and subtraction, Medium introduces multiplication, and Hard includes multi-step problems.
Yes! When creating or editing an alarm, tap "Repeat" and select the days of the week you want the alarm to repeat. You can select any combination of days.
Each snooze requires solving a math problem — there is no limit on the number of snoozes, but you must correctly solve a puzzle every time. This is by design to ensure you actually wake up!
Yes. Solve To Snooze works entirely offline. All alarms and math problems are generated on-device — no internet connection is ever required.
